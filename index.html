learning • building • writing

I enjoy working on problems where the goal isn’t obvious at first and the solution has to be built step by step. Most of my interest in technology comes from this process — understanding a system, breaking it down, and figuring out how things fit together under the hood.

I’m particularly drawn to areas that reward depth and clarity of thought: data structures and algorithms, competitive programming, and low-level or systems-oriented topics. I spend time on problem solving platforms like LeetCode, not just to “practice questions,” but to develop better ways of thinking about constraints, trade-offs, and efficiency.

At the moment, I’m still learning — and I’m intentional about that. I’m building my foundations in programming while gradually exploring deeper technical areas. I care more about understanding why something works than rushing through abstractions, and I prefer learning slowly but properly.
This space is where I document that process: things I’m learning, projects I’m working on, and ideas that feel worth revisiting. It’s less about showcasing finished outcomes and more about keeping a record of progress, curiosity, and effort.
